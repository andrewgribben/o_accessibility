O Captain! My Captain!

by Walt Whitman

    1. Line 1. O Captain! my Captain! our fearful trip is done;
    2. Line 2. The ship has weather'd every rack, the prize we sought is won;
    3. Line 3. The port is near, the bells I hear, the people all exulting,
    4. Line 4. While follow eyes the steady keel, the vessel grim and daring;
    5. Line 5. But O heart! heart! heart!
    6. Line 6. O the bleeding drops of red,
    7. Line 7. Where on the deck my Captain lies,
    8. Line 8. Fallen cold and dead.
    1. Line 9. O Captain! my Captain! rise up and hear the bells;
    2. Line 10. Rise up—for you the flag is flung—for you the bugle trills;
    3. Line 11. For you bouquets and ribbon'd wreaths—for you the shores a-crowding;
    4. Line 12. For you they call, the swaying mass, their eager faces turning;
    5. Line 13. Here Captain! dear father!
    6. Line 14. This arm beneath your head!
    7. Line 15. It is some dream that on the deck,
    8. Line 16. You've fallen cold and dead.
    1. Line 17. My Captain does not answer, his lips are pale and still;
    2. Line 18. My father does not feel my arm, he has no pulse nor will;
    3. Line 19. The ship is anchor'd safe and sound, its voyage closed and done;
    4. Line 20. From fearful trip the victor ship comes in with object won;
    5. Line 21. Exult, O shores, and ring, O bells!
    6. Line 22. But I, with mournful tread,
    7. Line 23. Walk the deck my Captain lies,
    8. Line 24. Fallen cold and dead.
